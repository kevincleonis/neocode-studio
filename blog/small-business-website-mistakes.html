7 Website Mistakes That Are Killing Your Small Business

We review hundreds of small business websites every year. These are the seven mistakes we see over and over again — and every single one of them is costing you money.

The frustrating thing about these mistakes is that most business owners do not even know they are making them. Your website might look "okay" to you, but to a potential customer comparing you against competitors, these issues scream unprofessional. Here are the seven biggest offenders.

#1 Expired or Missing SSL Certificate

If your website URL starts with "http://" instead of "https://", you have a problem. Browsers like Chrome now display a prominent "Not Secure" warning to visitors on sites without SSL. For a customer about to hand over their contact details or credit card information, this is an instant deal-breaker.

SSL certificates are often free through services like Let's Encrypt, and most hosting providers include them at no extra cost. There is genuinely no excuse for not having one in 2026. Beyond the trust factor, Google also uses HTTPS as a ranking signal, so you are hurting your SEO as well.

The fix: Contact your hosting provider and ask them to enable SSL. Most can do it within minutes. If they cannot, it is time for a new host.

#2 No Mobile Optimisation

This bears repeating because we still see it constantly: over 60% of your visitors are on phones, and if your site is not mobile-friendly, they are bouncing immediately. We are not talking about a site that "kind of works" on mobile — we mean a site that is genuinely easy and pleasant to use on a small screen.

Common issues include text that requires pinch-to-zoom, navigation menus that do not collapse properly, images that break the layout, and forms with tiny input fields that are painful to fill in on a phone.

The fix: Use Google's Mobile-Friendly Test to see how your site performs. If it fails, a responsive redesign should be your top priority. It is that important.

#3 Using Gmail as Your Business Email

Nothing says "I started this business last Tuesday in my spare room" quite like a contact email of johns-plumbing-services@gmail.com. A professional email address using your domain (e.g., john@johnsplumbing.co.uk) costs as little as £1 per month and immediately elevates the perception of your business.

Customers make snap judgements. A Gmail, Hotmail, or Yahoo email address on a business website erodes trust before you have even had a conversation. It also makes your emails more likely to land in spam folders when you are emailing potential clients.

The fix: Set up Google Workspace (£5.50/month) or use your hosting provider's included email service. Most domain registrars also offer email forwarding for free.

#4 No Customer Reviews or Testimonials

92% of consumers read online reviews before making a purchase or booking a service. If your website has zero social proof, you are asking visitors to take a leap of faith that most simply will not take — especially when your competitor's site is plastered with five-star Google reviews.

Even if you have great reviews on Google, Facebook, or Trustpilot, they need to be visible on your website too. Do not make potential customers go hunting for evidence that you are good at what you do.

The fix: Add your top 3-5 reviews to your homepage. Include the reviewer's name and the source (e.g., "Google Review"). If you do not have reviews yet, start asking happy customers. Most people are happy to leave one if you simply ask.

#5 Poor SEO Foundations

Having a beautiful website means nothing if nobody can find it. We regularly see small business sites with no meta descriptions, no heading hierarchy, duplicate title tags across every page, and zero local SEO setup.

If you are a local business and you have not claimed your Google Business Profile, set up location-specific pages, or included your address and service areas on your website, you are invisible to the people most likely to become customers — those searching for your services in your area.

The fix: At minimum, ensure every page has a unique title tag and meta description. Claim and fully complete your Google Business Profile. Include your location and service areas prominently on your site. These basics alone can dramatically improve your local visibility.

#6 Terrible Photos

Blurry photos taken on an old phone, generic stock images of handshakes and laptop screens, or worst of all — no images at all. Visual content is how people assess your business in seconds. Poor imagery makes even a well-designed site feel cheap and untrustworthy.

This is especially critical for businesses where the visual result matters: restaurants, salons, tradespeople, property services, and retail. If you cannot show high-quality examples of your work or products, customers will assume the quality matches the photos — low.

The fix: Invest in a professional photoshoot. A half-day shoot for a small business typically costs £200-£400 and gives you a library of images you can use for years across your website, social media, and marketing materials. It is one of the highest-ROI investments you can make.

#7 Missing or Buried Contact Information

You would be amazed how many business websites make it difficult to actually contact the business. Phone numbers hidden on a sub-page. No email address at all. A contact form that requires filling in 15 fields before you can send a simple enquiry. Some sites do not even have a contact page.

Every additional step between "I want to contact this business" and actually doing it is a step where you lose potential customers. The easier you make it, the more enquiries you get. It really is that simple.

The fix: Put your phone number and email in your website header or footer so they are visible on every page. Make your phone number clickable on mobile. Keep your contact form short — name, email, message is usually all you need. Consider adding a WhatsApp or live chat option for instant communication.

The Compound Effect

Here is the thing: most struggling websites are not making just one of these mistakes. They are making three, four, or all seven simultaneously. And the effect compounds. An expired SSL certificate plus no mobile optimisation plus a Gmail address creates an overall impression that your business is not to be trusted. No amount of great work will overcome that first impression.

Your website is your digital storefront. If your physical shop had a broken window, flickering lights, and no sign on the door, you would fix it immediately. Your website deserves the same attention.

The good news is that every one of these problems has a solution, and most are far less expensive than you think. The question is not whether you can afford to fix them — it is whether you can afford not to.
